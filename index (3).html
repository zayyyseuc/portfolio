CV

Chen Zai (b. 2004, China) is an environmental designer and interdisciplinary researcher currently pursuing a Bachelor of Arts at Nankai University, with an expected graduation in 2026. Her practice operates at the intersection of spatial design, human-computer interaction, and ecological systems thinking — asking how designed environments can listen, respond, and adapt to the people and ecologies that inhabit them.

Her work spans a broad range of media and methods: from data-driven urban analysis and VR spatial interfaces to physical computing installations and community co-creation processes. What connects these projects is a sustained interest in thresholds — the edges where the digital meets the material, where individual experience meets collective memory, and where design can quietly shift how people perceive their surroundings.

Chen Zai has been recognized with a National Second Prize for her Tianjin Salt Field Street Design, and her exhibition work has been permanently adopted by the Tianjin Science Museum. She has studied at the University of California, Berkeley (Summer 2024, GPA 3.9), where she deepened her engagement with HCI methodologies and participatory design frameworks.

She is currently open to graduate program opportunities and collaborative research projects in the fields of environmental design, spatial computing, and ecological interaction design.
