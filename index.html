CV

I'm an environmental designer trained to think across scales — from a room's materiality to a regional ecosystem. I'm completing a B.A. at Nankai University and joining the MDes program at UC Berkeley in Fall 2026.

My work spans ML sensing, VR spatial interfaces, and physical computing. The common thread is a question about gaps: between how a space is designed to function and how it is actually inhabited. My thesis takes this to a non-human extreme — a UE5 prototype that models habitat cognition from the perspectives of deer, squirrel, and ant.

I'm moving toward healthcare environments for aging populations, where that gap tends to matter most.
