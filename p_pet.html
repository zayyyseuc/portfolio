Pet Space

A non-intrusive remote companionship system that helps burnt-out pet owners feel their pet's presence without turning home into a surveillance interface.

Design Position

Technology should stay at the periphery of attention. Pet Space builds an "affective presence" channel through location, state, and short-form audio cues—so users can feel relief, stability, and care without cameras, endless screens, or privacy anxiety.

Burnout turns companionship into a broken signal.

Pet Space asks a simple question: how can remote pet tech restore emotional connection without becoming surveillance?

Perceptual bonding replaces active monitoring.

Pet status becomes an emotional anchor: enough signal to reassure, not enough noise to distract.

Designing a gentle feedback loop.

A four-step service loop compresses complex sensing into one calm companionship moment.

One system, four gentle moves.

The strategy is hierarchical: first sense, then compress, then reassure, then optionally connect.
SenseInfrared + radar detect room-level presence.
TranslateActivity becomes route, status, anomaly cues.
ReassureA dedicated terminal enables calm check-ins.
ConnectShort audio plays where the pet actually is.
StressUser seeks instant emotional relief.
CheckSees room and route in one glance.
ReachSends soft audio into the right room.
ResetReturns to work with more certainty.

Three modules turn the concept into a working prototype.

The prototype condenses into three modules: detect, relay, reassure.

Detect → relay → reassure.

The prototype matters because the system architecture directly shapes the emotional experience.
Detection nodes
  • Infrared + radar improve reliability.
  • Each node maps to a room or zone.
  • Speaker enables local audio playback.
→
ESP32-C3 gateway
  • Aggregates room data.
  • Routes commands to nodes.
  • Maintains history and anomaly checks.
→
Portable terminal
  • Shows room, route, and status.
  • Triggers room-specific audio.
  • Feels calmer than a phone app.
Prototype value
