A Passive Biometric Monitoring System for Early Stroke Detection via Bilateral Facial EMG Asymmetry Analysis.

Project Vision

By leveraging non-invasive daily physiological monitoring, the critical window for stroke detection extends beyond emergency rooms into home environments — establishing an invisible lifeline for high-risk individuals living alone.

Research Background

Against the backdrop of an aging society, millions of vulnerable individuals living alone face immense survival challenges. This research investigates the critical gap between stroke onset and medical intervention — a window determined by the speed of detection.

Key Pain Points

  • Proactive testing devices require conscious action at the moment of crisis — impossible during stroke onset with cognitive impairment
  • Smart watches focus on heart rate and step counts, lacking specificity for neurovascular events
  • Existing home care services are fragmented and high-cost, inaccessible to solitary elderly residents
  • FAST principle awareness is low among high-risk populations — facial asymmetry is the earliest, most prominent sign

Insight

Proactive testing has become a mandatory requirement — passive biometric sensing that continuously monitors without user awareness or effort is the only viable solution for this population.

Design Strategy

The core design question: how do we create a monitoring system that is invisible to the user, yet precise enough to catch early stroke signals? The answer lies in reframing surveillance as companionship — and presence as protection.

01
Passive Sensing
No buttons. No screens. Dual-channel EMG electrodes attached bilaterally to zygomaticus major — the muscle activated by all facial expressions.
02
Continuous Baseline
System learns each user's personal symmetry baseline over 72 hours, adapting to natural variations in facial muscle activation patterns.
03
Asymmetry Detection
CNN-LSTM model compares bilateral signals in real time. Significant deviation from baseline triggers a cascading alert response.
04
Silent Alert
Automatic notification to caregivers and emergency contacts within 100ms of detection — no user action required.
vs. Smart Watch
Specificity Gap

Apple Watch achieves broad cardiovascular monitoring but no specificity for neurovascular events. Our EMG approach targets the exact biological marker of stroke onset.

vs. PERS Button
Activation Barrier

Personal Emergency Response Systems require conscious, physical activation — impossible when stroke causes motor and cognitive impairment simultaneously.

vs. Voice Assistant
Aphasia Problem

Stroke commonly causes aphasia — loss of speech. Voice-activated systems fail at precisely the moment they are needed most.

Technical Implementation

A dual-path machine learning architecture processes simultaneous audio and visual signals from bilateral facial EMG channels, achieving 94.2% real-time accuracy through a validated CNN-LSTM neural network.

94%
Weighted accuracy — real-time CNN-LSTM stroke detection model
100ms
Alert latency from detection to caregiver notification
16kHz
EMG signal sampling rate via STM32 with serial transmission at 2Mbps

Hardware Stack

  • Arduino Uno — signal acquisition controller + TFT display output
  • Dual analog inputs A0/A1 — bilateral EMG electrode channels
  • STM32 — 16kHz audio acquisition at 2Mbps baud rate serial transmission
  • Servo motor — physical button control switching 0° to 180°
  • Red/Green/Yellow electrode placement: zygomaticus major (positive/negative) + ear grounding

ML Architecture

  • 22-dimensional feature space capturing bilateral EMG asymmetry characteristics
  • CNN path: image-based spatial feature extraction from signal spectrograms
  • LSTM path: temporal sequence modeling for activity pattern recognition
  • Adaptive noise filtering — 20Hz notch filter + 35Hz Butterworth bandpass
  • Training set accuracy 97.5% · Resting state detection 98.8%

Installation & Deployment

The validated fusion architecture is translated into a compact, battery-powered sensing unit — a portable device integrating synchronized image capture and acoustic recording with onboard preprocessing for authentic field deployment.

Form Factor
Wearable Headset

Lightweight bilateral electrode headset designed for passive wear during sleep and stationary activity periods. No active management required by the user after initial calibration.

Electrode Placement
Zygomaticus Protocol

Electroencephalography testing identified zygomaticus major as the most effective location. Red electrode on positive, green on negative, yellow behind ear as grounding reference.

Alert System
Multi-Channel Notification

Simultaneous alert dispatch to registered caregivers, family contacts, and emergency services with location data and detection confidence score.

Validation Results

  • Resting state detection: 98.8% accuracy
  • Normal facial movements: 93.8% accuracy
  • Left-side muscle exertion: 96.4% accuracy
  • Right-side muscle exertion: 84.2% accuracy
  • Clinical specificity: 93.3% · Sensitivity: 92.8%

Application Scenarios

  • Home monitoring for high-risk individuals living alone
  • Post-stroke rehabilitation progress assessment
  • Emergency supplementary measures for community and institutional elderly care
  • Portable health monitoring terminal integration
